  1. "AI in Education." Intel, 2023, www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/learn/ ai-in-education.html. This article, written by Intel Corporation, a technology company that produces computer architecture, summarizes many benefits and drawbacks of AI in the education landscape. This article brings up several statistical benefits related to the use of AI, especially in the student landscape. This article also discusses the many shortcomings of AI in the classroom, especially the potential biases that AI has due to its limitations in training capabilities. This article is a secondary source due to its analysis of a survey conducted by themselves. This source provides information on how AI can be used to further education for students as well as the disadvantages to the use of AI, which can be used to help answer both of my questions in the evidence section. This source is more on the border of credibility due to intel's potential business opportunities that could be obtained by promoting AI, but due to their admission of AI's shortcomings and their position as an important technology and research corporation, it is a credible source.
  2. Association, National Education. "III. The Current State of Artificial Intelligence in Education | NEA." Www.nea.org, 2025, www.nea.org/ resource-library/artificial-intelligence-education/ iii-current-state-artificial-intelligence-education. This article, written by NEA or the National Education Association discusses guidelines that states such as Arizona and California have instated in order to regulate the use of AI for education purposes. The article presents the three. categories of AI, reactive, predictive , and generative AIs, as well as explaining them and their uses in education. They also explain what types of AI are useful for each group of individuals in education, from the students to the institutio n. This secondary source, due to its compilation of surveys and self formulated thoughts, is written by NEA, which is a group that fights for education rights and the educators, while also trying to keep the community informed about where the tax payer dollars go when it comes to education. This source provides valuable information about AI and how it often gets used for appropriate education purposes that I need in order to write about how to safely use AI for education. This source will likely be used in my background and my evidence for my question.

  4. Klimova, Blanka, and Marcel Pikhart. "Exploring the Effects of Artificial Intelligence on Student and Academic Well-Being in Higher Education: A Mini-Review." Frontiers in Psychology, vol. 16, no. 16, 3 Feb. 2025, pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11830699/, https://doi.org/10.3389/ fpsyg.2025.1498132. This research paper written by Blanka Klimova and Marcel Pikhart discusses the results of a survey they ran on college students about AI and its uses for educational purposes. While also discussing many similar advantages and disadvantages to the other sources used for this project, this article uniquely discusses AI and its mental health effects on students in higher education. The survey shows that due to AI's capability to reduce overall workload, students who use AI for educational purposes generally are happier and less stressed. This secondary source, written for Frontiers in Psychology by Klimova and Pikhar, shows very little if any bias due to its very statistical analysis of collected data, going as far as to explain their methodology and discuss the background of the situation. Both of the authors have experience writing about large language models as well as education, making them very qualified to write and conduct surveys on this topic. This source helps to prove the lesser known mental health advantages and disadvantages of AI which helps to provide more evidence to answer my research questions, while providing a new topic to talk about rather than the strict education uses.
  5. Silvestrone, Shallon, and Jillian Rubman. "AI-Assisted Grading: A Magic Wand or a Pandora's Box?" MIT Sloan Teaching & Learning Technologies, 9 May 2024, mitsloanedtech.mit.edu/2024/05/09/ ai-assisted-grading-a-magic-wand-or-a-pandoras-box/. Instead of coming from a primarily students perspective, it talks about the use of AI for teaching, especially in the grading category of education. They discuss the advantages and disadvantages of AI grading, including the reduction of workload, which can be used to do anything else, as well as the harshness and biases of AI grading. Importantly, they reference how AI does not understand the nuances of the real world, which makes assignments related to real world applications of knowledge unfair to grade with AI. It is a secondary source as it is a compilation of some statements and thoughts professors have about AI for grading purposes.This article is very credible because it is published by MIT, a very reputable source in both education and technology, demonstrating their credibility. This source provides evidence for my research questions that comes from a different than normal perspective than usually shared, and thus will be placed and used in the evidence section of my project.
